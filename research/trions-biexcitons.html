Featured result

Equilibrium Fluids of Excitonic Ions and Molecules

Interlayer trions and trilayer biexcitons extend electron-hole bilayers from neutral exciton fluids to richer mixtures of charged and molecular excitonic quasiparticles.

Research Question

What new equilibrium quasiparticles appear when an excitonic insulator is doped with excess charge carriers or extended into multilayer electron-hole-electron structures?

This question is motivated by an analogy to atomic matter: electrons and nuclei form atoms, ions, and molecules, which then assemble into a wide range of collective phases. Electron-hole systems offer a solid-state route to a parallel hierarchy of excitonic matter.

Key Result

In electron-hole bilayers, excess carriers bind to interlayer excitons to form charged interlayer trions, creating an electrically tunable fluid of excitonic ions. In electron-hole-electron trilayers, two electrons and two holes can form neutral trilayer biexcitons, an excitonic-molecule analogue.

Together, these results show that van der Waals electron-hole devices can host more than one kind of composite particle. They provide a route toward tunable mixtures of excitons, trions, biexcitons, and charge carriers with strong Coulomb correlations.
