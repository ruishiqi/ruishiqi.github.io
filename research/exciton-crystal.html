Featured result

Exciton Crystal

A moiré excitonic insulator can organize composite bosonic quasiparticles into a crystalline phase driven by interactions.

Research Question

Can excitons, which are electrically neutral composite bosons, form crystalline order when placed in a periodic moiré potential and coupled by strong dipolar interactions?

Moiré materials provide a natural lattice for correlated quantum matter. When combined with electron-hole pairing, they allow excitonic phases to be studied in a regime where density, interactions, and lattice filling are all experimentally tunable.

Key Result

This work observed an exciton crystal phase in a moiré excitonic insulator. The phase reflects interaction-driven ordering of composite bosonic quasiparticles rather than the localization of ordinary electrons alone.

The result connects excitonic insulators to a broader landscape of ordered bosonic matter, including crystalline states, condensates, and multicomponent fluids that can emerge from the same electron-hole platform.
