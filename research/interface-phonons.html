Interface Phonons at the Nanoscale

Electron energy-loss spectroscopy can resolve phonon dispersion near buried interfaces with nanometer-scale spatial precision.

Research Question

How do lattice vibrations behave at interfaces, where local bonding, symmetry, and dielectric environment differ from the bulk crystal?

Interface phonons influence heat transport, polaritonic response, and electron-phonon coupling, but their momentum-resolved behavior is difficult to access with conventional optical or thermal probes.

Key Result

This work used momentum-resolved electron energy-loss spectroscopy to directly measure phonon dispersion at an interface with nanometer spatial resolution. The method connects real-space localization with reciprocal-space vibrational information in a single nanoscale experiment.

The approach helped establish four-dimensional vibrational spectroscopy as a tool for probing local lattice dynamics in nanostructures, interfaces, and low-dimensional materials.
