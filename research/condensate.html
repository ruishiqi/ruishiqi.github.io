Featured result

Two-Component Exciton Condensates

An equilibrium exciton fluid can condense into a multicomponent ordered state whose internal structure is switchable with a weak magnetic field.

Research Question

Can an electron-hole bilayer host an equilibrium exciton condensate, and if so, what internal degrees of freedom does the condensate order carry?

In transition-metal dichalcogenide bilayers, excitons inherit valley, spin, and layer structure from the underlying bands. This creates the possibility of condensates that are not single-component scalar fluids, but multicomponent quantum orders.

Key Result

This work observed a two-component exciton condensate in an equilibrium exciton fluid. The condensate exhibits spinor order and can be switched among competing condensate states by a weak magnetic field.

The result points toward a flexible platform for studying multicomponent condensates in solids, where internal quantum numbers, density, and magnetic field can all be controlled in the same device.
